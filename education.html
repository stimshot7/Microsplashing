Urinal Dynamics

What Is Microsplashing?

Microsplashing is the splashback of a person's own urine that results from the improper use of an urinal, causing disgusting toilets with scattered drops on the floor, and an embarrasing dark spot on the person's pants.

What Are The Risks of Microsplashing?

According to Doctor Tad Truscott, a reasearcher and a professor of mechanical engineer at the Brigham Young University emphasized that the toilets and bathrooms with urine puddles on the floor are a breeding ground for bacteria.

Techniques To Avoid Microsplashing

Prof Tadd Truscott and Randy Hurd of the "Splash Lab" at Brigham Young University in Provo, Utah, suggest the following techniques

  • Getting as close as possible - a smooth unbroken stream is better than droplets
  • Angling the stream - aiming sideways or downwards instead of straight at the toilet water/wall
  • Placing toilet tissue in the bowl to soften impact
  • Sitting down instead of standing
