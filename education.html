What Is Microsplashing?

Microsplashing is the splashback of a person's own urine caused when the urine stream hits the toilet or urinal bowl at an improper angle or distance, thus resulting in disgusting toilets with scattered drops on the floor, or an embarrasing dark spot on the person's pants.

What Are The Risks
of Microsplashing?

According to Doctor Tad Truscott, a researcher and a professor of mechanical
engineer at the Brigham Young University stated that toilets and bathrooms
with urine puddles on the floor are a breeding ground for bacteria.

Techniques To Avoid Microsplashing

Prof Tadd Truscott and Randy Hurd of the "Splash Lab" at Brigham Young University in
Provo, Utah, suggest the following techniques:

  • Get closer to the toilet or to the urinal bowl as an unbroken stream is better than droplets
  • Angle the urine stream to deflect the urinal spray
  • Place toilet tissue in the bowl to minimize the impact
  • The best way to prevent microsplashing is to sit down instead of standing
